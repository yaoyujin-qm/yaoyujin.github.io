About

Hi! I’m Yaoyu Jin. I am currently a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Northeastern University, where I am supervised by Prof. Xiaochun Yang, and a joint PhD student at Queen Mary University of London, where I am supervised by Dr. Ziquan Liu and Dr. Shanxin Yuan.

My research focuses on trustworthy artificial intelligence, particularly in large language models and generative models. I am interested in understanding how model behaviors emerge and how we can improve their reliability, robustness, and safety. My recent work studies knowledge editing in large language models, exploring how edited knowledge survives downstream fine-tuning and how to improve the robustness of model editing through context selection and optimization strategies.

I am also interested in copyright protection for AI-generated content, including detecting the reproduction of copyrighted characters, artistic styles, and visual elements in generative models. My goal is to develop practical methods that help build more trustworthy and responsible AI systems.

Before starting my PhD, I received my M.Sc. in Computer Science and B.Sc. in Applied Statistics from Northeastern University.

Outside of research, I enjoy traveling, photography, and exploring different cultures and cities.
